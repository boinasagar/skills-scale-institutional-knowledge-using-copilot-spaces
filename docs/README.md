# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management Documentation! This is your central hub for understanding how we run projects, collaborate across teams, and deliver value consistently and transparently.

## Overview

OctoAcme's project management processes are structured to provide clarity, consistency, and effective collaboration across cross-functional teams. At the core, projects follow a lifecycle beginning with clear initiation—where the business need, stakeholders, and success metrics are defined—progressing through detailed planning, rigorous execution, structured release, and ending with retrospective analysis for continuous improvement. This lifecycle is supported by concise templates and checklists to ensure all critical steps and deliverables, such as Project One-pagers, risk registers, and release notes, are systematically addressed and documented.

The team structure emphasizes defined roles with clear responsibilities: Project Managers (PMs) coordinate delivery, schedules, and risks; Product Managers (PdMs) set goals, prioritize backlogs, and measure outcomes; Developers implement features, maintain code quality, and contribute to planning; QA/Testers validate deliverables against acceptance criteria. Each role's goals and typical communication channels are outlined, ensuring that every contributor understands their impact and accountability within the wider process.

Communication is intentionally frequent and multi-layered to maintain alignment and transparency. Daily standups focus on short-term progress and blockers, weekly delivery syncs cover broader updates and risks, and monthly stakeholder updates keep leadership informed. Key artifacts like project boards, risk registers, and status updates serve as single sources of truth, while escalation paths and incident communication templates provide guidance for urgent issues or incidents.

Quality assurance is woven throughout the process: automated testing, linting, and security scans are required before code merges; manual QA and end-to-end smoke tests safeguard critical flows before releases; and all deployments follow a documented checklist. Continuous improvement is fostered through regular retrospectives, where what went well and areas for growth are discussed, and actionable improvements are tracked and measured, ensuring that the team not only delivers but evolves its practices over time.
