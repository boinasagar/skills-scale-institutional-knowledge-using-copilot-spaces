# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile practices. They serve the team by ensuring smooth workflow and continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and impediments that slow team progress
- Coach team members on agile practices and self-organization
- Shield the team from external distractions and interruptions
- Track team metrics (velocity, burndown) and identify improvement opportunities

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Ensure agile practices are followed effectively

### Typical Communication
- Facilitating all scrum ceremonies
- One-on-ones with team members to identify impediments
- Coordination with Project Manager on cross-team dependencies

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates on timeline management, escalates impediments, aligns on resource needs
- **Developers**: Coaches on agile practices, removes blockers, facilitates team ceremonies
- **Product Manager**: Ensures backlog is refined and ready for planning, facilitates stakeholder communication

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers lead user research and design user experiences and interfaces. They ensure products are intuitive, accessible, and delightful for users.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Define information architecture and interaction patterns
- Establish and maintain design systems and guidelines
- Validate designs through user feedback and metrics

### Goals
- Create intuitive and accessible user experiences
- Ensure design consistency across products
- Validate design decisions with user research

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critiques
- User research findings and insights presentations
- Design handoff sessions with developers

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on requirements and user needs, validates design solutions against business goals
- **Developers**: Works together on implementation feasibility, provides design specs and assets
- **QA Automation Engineer**: Partners on usability validation and accessibility testing

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure, and deployment automation. They enable reliable and efficient software delivery through automation and monitoring.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure as code and cloud resources
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Automate deployment processes and rollback procedures
- Ensure security and compliance in the delivery pipeline

### Goals
- Minimize deployment time and manual intervention
- Maximize system reliability and uptime
- Improve observability and incident response

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure design reviews
- Incident postmortems and runbooks
- Release planning and deployment coordination

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on build processes, provides deployment tools and infrastructure access
- **QA Automation Engineer**: Integrates automated tests into CI/CD pipeline
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on release planning, provides deployment status and metrics

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts gather, analyze, and report metrics on product usage and success. They enable data-informed decision-making through insights and visualizations.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key product metrics and KPIs
- Analyze user behavior and product performance
- Create dashboards and reports for stakeholders
- Conduct A/B tests and experimental analysis
- Identify trends and provide actionable insights

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision-making
- Measure product success and user satisfaction
- Identify opportunities for improvement through data

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metrics reviews and insights presentations
- Ad-hoc analysis requests and findings
- Dashboard reviews with stakeholders

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Partners on defining success metrics, provides analysis to inform prioritization
- **Project Manager**: Delivers metrics on project progress and outcomes
- **Developers**: Collaborates on instrumentation and data collection requirements

---

## QA Automation Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Automation Engineers develop and maintain automated test suites for rapid validation. They ensure quality through comprehensive automated testing strategies.

### Responsibilities
- Design and implement automated test frameworks
- Create and maintain automated test suites (unit, integration, E2E)
- Integrate tests into CI/CD pipelines
- Identify and report defects with clear reproduction steps
- Define test coverage goals and track quality metrics

### Goals
- Maximize test coverage and reliability
- Minimize manual testing effort through automation
- Catch defects early in the development cycle

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews and coverage discussions
- Bug triage meetings and defect reports
- Automation framework demos and training

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on testability and test coverage, reviews code changes for test impact
- **DevOps Engineer**: Integrates automated tests into CI/CD pipelines
- **UX Designer**: Partners on usability and accessibility testing

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts elicit business requirements and translate them into actionable user stories and acceptance criteria. They bridge the gap between business stakeholders and technical teams.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements
- Translate requirements into user stories with clear acceptance criteria
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Create process flows and business requirement documents
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business needs

### Goals
- Ensure clear understanding of business requirements
- Bridge communication between business and technical teams
- Validate that solutions deliver expected business value

### Typical Communication
- Requirements gathering sessions with stakeholders
- User story refinement and backlog grooming
- Solution validation and acceptance reviews

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on requirements elicitation, ensures alignment of business needs with product vision
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on scope definition, helps manage stakeholder expectations
- **Developers**: Clarifies requirements and acceptance criteria, validates implementation
